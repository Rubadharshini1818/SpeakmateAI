Get over

verb
/ɡet ˈəʊvə/

Primary Definition

To recover from an illness, loss, or difficulty; to overcome a mental or emotional challenge.

Multiple Meanings

1

Overcoming feelings: "It took her a long time to get over her breakup."

2

Physical recovery: "He’s still trying to get over a nasty cold."

3

Disbelief: "I just can't get over how much she's grown!"

Memory Tip

Think of "Over" as a fence. You are climbing over the obstacle (illness or sadness) to reach the other side (health or happiness).

Word Family

Noun Getover (slang)
Verb Get over
Adjective Overable (rare)

check_circle Usage in Context

Scenario Correct Sentence Explanation
Illness "It took her a week to get over the flu." Refers to physical recovery.
Emotional "You need to get over your fear of failure." Managing mental barriers.
Surprise "I can't get over how cheap this is!" Expression of astonishment.
Grief "He never truly got over losing his dog." Lingering emotional pain.
Problems "Once we get over this hurdle, we're set." Practical obstacle.

Synonyms

Recover Overcome Heal Conquer

Antonyms
